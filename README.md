# 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis of Metal Price Trends (1992–2021)

---

## 🧾 Project Overview

This project explores **30 years of historical metal price data** to uncover meaningful patterns in commodity markets.  
It focuses on five major metals: **Aluminium, Gold, Nickel, Silver, and Uranium**.

The goal is to transform raw financial data into **clear, actionable insights** that can support:

- Investment decisions  
- Economic forecasting  
- Industrial and supply chain planning  

---

## 🎯 Objective

- Analyze long-term price trends of major industrial and precious metals  
- Compare behavior across different commodities  
- Study the impact of inflation on real prices  
- Identify volatility patterns and market disruptions  
- Extract business and economic insights from historical data  

---

## 📁 Dataset Information

**Source:** Kaggle – Metal Price Changes (Last 30 Years)  
**Time Period:** 1992 – 2021  
**Dataset Size:** 361 rows × 13 columns  

**Key Features:**

- Monthly prices of Aluminium, Gold, Nickel, Silver, Uranium  
- Inflation rate  
- Inflation-adjusted price values  

---

## 🛠️ Technologies Used

- Python – Core programming language  
- Pandas – Data cleaning and manipulation  
- NumPy – Numerical computations  
- Matplotlib – Data visualization  
- Jupyter Notebook – Analysis environment  

---

## 🧹 Data Preprocessing

To ensure clean and reliable analysis, the following steps were performed:

- Handled missing values using Forward Fill (ffill) and interpolation  
- Created a unified Date column for time-series analysis  
- Verified dataset consistency and structure  
- Selected relevant features for focused analysis  

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### 📈 Trend Analysis

- Studied long-term price movements across all metals  
- Compared growth patterns and market behavior  

---

### 📉 Volatility Analysis

- Identified highly volatile metals  
- Detected periods of market instability  

---

### 💰 Inflation Impact Analysis

- Compared nominal vs inflation-adjusted prices  
- Evaluated real purchasing power over time  

---

### 📊 Statistical Summary

- Mean, median, and standard deviation analysis  
- Distribution patterns and variability insights  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Gold shows a strong long-term upward trend and acts as a reliable inflation hedge  
- Silver is significantly more volatile than gold  
- Uranium experienced extreme spikes, especially around 2007–2008  
- Aluminium and Nickel closely reflect industrial demand cycles  
- Inflation significantly changes the interpretation of long-term growth
